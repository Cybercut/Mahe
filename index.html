Welcome To Mahe

History of Mahe

Mahe, also known as Mayyazhi, is a small town and a district of the Union Territory of Puducherry. It is situated at the mouth of the Mahé River and is surrounded by the State of Kerala.

Etymology

The name Mahé comes from Mayyazhi, the name given to the local river in the Malayalam language. The original spelling found on French documents from the early 1720s is Mayé, with Mahé and Mahié also found on documents, maps and geographical dictionaries until the early 19th century. It was also believed the town is named after Bertrand François Mahé de La Bourdonnais, an officer working for the French East India Company.

Demographics

As of 2011 India census,the total population of Mahe is 41,816, predominantly Malayalis and some guest workers from states like Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. Mahé has an average literacy rate of 98%. The share of Hindus in population is 67% and Muslims are 30% as per 2011 Census of India. Christians comprise 3% of the population.

History and Administration

Mahe was still a French colony even after the Independence of India in 1947. After a long anti-colonial struggle, it finally joined the Indian Union on 13th June 1954. .The area of Mahé begins from Mayyazhi puzha in the north to Azhiyoor at the south. Mahé consists of Mahé town and Naluthara, which includes four villages:

  • Pandakkal
  • Palloor
  • Chalakkara
  • Chembra

Agriculture

Some of the major crops grown in Mahe are Coconuts, Arecanuts, Pepper, Bananas, Tuber Crops, Mangoes, Jackfruits and Tapioca.

Culture

Some of the famous events in Mahe are Theyyam and St. Theresa's Church Festival.
